Town profiles & train connections

Badge = province code · FR = south of Rome (Ciociaria) · XX = north of / around Rome.

Narni Umbria · ~19,000

Hilltop medieval town on a ridge above the Nera gorge; well-preserved old core, Narni Sotterranea. The one you already like.

Rome: 47 min–1h20 direct · Terni: ~10 min · Florence: ~2h30 (change Orte/Terni)
Station→center: Narni Scalo ~2–3 km below; "Narni Link" shuttle 10–15 min or taxi.
Seismic zone 3 (lower) — central Italy, so not zero.

Orte Lazio · ~9,000

Small Etruscan hill town above the Tiber; cheapest market, best rail links.

Rome: 33–42 min direct (~27/day) · Florence: ~1h30–2h · major junction
Station→center: Orte Scalo ~2.5–3 km below; bus/taxi up.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Spoleto Umbria · ~37,000

Art & festival town; Roman roots, hilltop Duomo, Rocca, Ponte delle Torri.

Rome: 1h09–1h36 direct · Foligno: ~15 min · Perugia: ~1h (change Foligno)
Station→center: ~1 km flat walk, or bus to the Posterna escalators.
⚠ HIGH seismic zone (zona 1) — active Apennine belt; insist on structural / anti-seismic checks, especially old masonry.

Foligno Umbria · ~57,000

Flat valley hub; lively historic core around Piazza della Repubblica. Most convenient of all.

Rome: 1h28–1h49 direct · Perugia: 31–39 min · Ancona: direct · key junction
Station→center: ~600 m / 8 min flat walk — no bus.
⚠ HIGH seismic zone (zona 1) — active Apennine belt; insist on structural / anti-seismic checks, especially old masonry.

Orvieto Umbria · ~20,000

Clifftop tufa town with a great Gothic cathedral; touristy, pricier.

Rome: 1h–1h33 direct · Florence: ~2h · Florence–Rome "lenta" line
Station→center: Orvieto Scalo at cliff foot; funicular + minibus.
Seismic zone 3 (lower) — central Italy, so not zero.

Chiusi Tuscany · ~8,500

Ancient Etruscan hilltown; museum, catacombs; sleepy old center.

Rome: 1h21–2h18 · Florence: 1h26–2h · Siena-branch junction
Station→center: Chiusi Scalo ~1.6 km below; city bus ~5 min.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Terni Umbria · ~110,000

Larger working city; gateway to Marmore Falls. Modest centro, cheap stock.

Rome: 59 min–1h19 direct · Spoleto/Foligno: up the line
Station→center: central; 9–12 min to centro edge, ~20 min to the heart. Walkable.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Perugia Umbria capital · ~165,000

Big hilltop university city; magnificent historic center, most amenities — toughest budget.

Rome: ~2h–2h40 (change Foligno) · Florence: ~1h25–2h (change Terontola)
Station→center: Fontivegge in the valley; MiniMetrò to Pincetto ~10–12 min.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Spello Umbria · ~8,500

Exquisite Roman-medieval hill town (the Infiorata flower festival); one of Italy's "most beautiful villages."

Rome: ~1h51 (~13/day) · Foligno: ~6 min · Assisi/Perugia: short hop
Station→center: at the foot of the town, ~1 km (short walk/climb). Note: little in-budget stock found.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Trevi Umbria · ~8,000

Dramatic hilltop town wrapped in olive groves above the Clitunno plain.

Rome: ~1h45–2h · Foligno: ~5 min (Rome–Ancona line)
Station→center: in Borgo Trevi (plain) ~3–4 km below; bus/taxi up. Hilltop centro stock thin; most listings in valley frazioni.
⚠ HIGH seismic zone (zona 1) — active Apennine belt; insist on structural / anti-seismic checks, especially old masonry.

Castiglione del Lago Umbria · ~15,000

Walled town on a promontory over Lake Trasimeno.

Florence: 1h14–1h52 (~20/day) · Rome: ~2h (change Terontola/Chiusi) · Terontola–Chiusi line
Station→center: near the lake, ~10–15 min to the old town. Note: in-budget stock is in the lake frazioni (Macchie, San Fatucchio, Porto…), not the walled centro.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Città della Pieve Umbria · ~7,500

Mellow brick hill town, birthplace of Perugino; affordable.

Rome: ~1h45–2h · Florence: ~1h45 · also served by Chiusi–Chianciano
Station→center: own station ~3.4 km below (bus). Centro stock mostly 1-bed or to-renovate.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Castiglion Fiorentino Tuscany (Arezzo) · ~13,000

Handsome walled Tuscan town between Arezzo and Cortona — a cheaper alternative to Cortona.

Florence: ~1h05 (~21/day) · Rome: ~2h25 (7 direct) · Florence–Arezzo–Rome line
Station→center: near the centre, walkable. Centro stock exists but mostly 1-bedroom.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Poggio Mirteto Lazio · Sabina · ~6,000

Quiet Sabina hill town in the Tiber valley; cheapest of the new set, good 2–3BR centro supply.

Rome (Tiburtina): ~48 min, very frequent (FL1/regional, ~23/day)
Station→center: Poggio Mirteto Scalo is in the valley; the borgo is up the hill via the "Poggio Mirteto Link" bus.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Todi Umbria · ~16,000

Iconic hilltop town — Piazza del Popolo, Tempio della Consolazione. Rich, cheap centro storico stock.

⚠ Ex-FCU: rail suspended; Terni–Perugia section funded to reopen ~2026–2027. For now: bus/car, or mainline via Orte/Foligno + connection.
Station→center: Todi Ponte Rio/Ponte Naia in the valley; bus up.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Deruta Umbria · ~9,000

Famous maiolica-ceramics town on a low hill south of Perugia. A couple of cheap centro bilocali.

⚠ Ex-FCU: same reopening caveat as Todi (~2026–2027).
Station→center: FCU station near the lower town; bus/car meanwhile.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Massa Martana Umbria · ~3,800

Small walled medieval town SE of Todi — surprisingly deep, cheap centro storico 2–3BR supply.

⚠ Ex-FCU: same reopening caveat (~2026–2027); served meanwhile by Todi/regional bus.
Station→center: small FCU halt; car/bus for now.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Umbertide Umbria · ~16,000

Tiber-valley town with the Rocca, Alta Valle del Tevere.

⚠ Ex-FCU (north): runs but works-suspended (buses) ~14 Jun–6 Sep 2026; electrified service from 2026.
Station→center: in town (flat). Note: essentially no sub-€90k apartments — market is villas/casali.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Città di Castello Umbria · ~38,000

Renaissance art town in the upper Tiber valley.

⚠ Ex-FCU (north): works suspension (buses) ~14 Jun–6 Sep 2026; service otherwise.
Station→center: in town (flat). Note: very little sub-€90k stock; centro apartments start ~€129k.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Tivoli Lazio (Roma) · ~56,000

Famous for Villa d'Este & Hadrian's Villa (UNESCO); historic center on a hillside above the Aniene falls. The rare walk-to-train town — but pricier & touristy.

Rome (Tiburtina): ~40–60 min, hourly (FL2 regional)
Station→center: ~0.5 km, walkable. Much stock is in Villa Adriana / Tivoli Terme / Bivio San Polo (outlying); true centro thinner.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Rieti Lazio capital · ~47,000

"Centre of Italy"; pleasant historic center on the Velino, very affordable with abundant cheap stock.

Rome: direct ~1h47, ~11/day (secondary Terni line — slower/less frequent than the FL lines)
Station→center: near the city. Lots of cheap flats but mostly modern-city — verify which are truly centro storico (Terminillo/Pian de' Valli = mountain frazioni).
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — Rieti province's NE (Amatrice area) is zona 1; the city is zona 2. Have the structure assessed.

Anagni Lazio (Frosinone) · ~21,000

"City of Popes" — a strikingly intact medieval hill town (Boniface VIII, the cathedral) in Ciociaria. Exceptionally cheap, very deep centro storico stock.

Rome (Termini): ~50 min, hourly, ~€5 (FL6) · Naples: ~2h (change at Cassino) · Frosinone: ~20 min
Station→center: ⚠ Anagni-Fiuggi is ~6–8 km below the town — shuttle bus up (bigger gap than usual).
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Ferentino Lazio (Frosinone) · ~20,000

Ancient pre-Roman hill town with cyclopean walls and a hilltop duomo. Very cheap; many large centro apartments.

Rome: ~55 min, hourly (FL6; station Ferentino-Supino in the valley) · Naples: ~1h45–2h (via Cassino) · Frosinone: ~12 min
Station→center: station below the hill — bus/car up.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Frosinone Lazio · Ciociaria capital · ~44,000

Provincial capital: historic upper town on a hill, larger modern lower town. Cheap stock, but the audit showed it's mostly in the lower modern city — the hilltop old town has very little.

Rome (Termini): ~45–60 min, hourly · Naples: ~1h10–2h, frequent (on the Rome–Cassino–Naples line) · Cassino: ~35 min (FL6). Note: FL6 has a works closure ~2–31 Aug 2026 (after your trip).
Station→center: station in the lower town; upper historic town by bus/escalators.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.

Amelia Umbria (Terni) · ~11,000

One of Italy's oldest towns — monumental pre-Roman polygonal walls, the bronze Germanicus, an atmospheric upper old town. Very cheap, the deepest centro-storico stock of the set.

⚠ No own station — nearest is Narni–Amelia (~10–12 km away, in Narni Scalo) on the Rome–Ancona line, or Orte. Linked only by a sparse Busitalia bus (line E631, ~7 weekday runs, minimal weekends) — effectively car-dependent.
Note: much of the cheap "Centro" stock is the Via delle Rimembranze axis just below the walls (tagged Central); true inside-the-walls units are tagged Centro storico.
⚠ Seismic zone 2 (moderate-high) — have the structure assessed.
